# GRACEFELL v2.11.2 release record

## Outcome

Three rapid ATK presses now reliably produce the complete three-hit light combo on phone and
desktop. The third slash remains the stronger light finisher but no longer sounds or reads like
HVY.

## Root cause

`Input.pressed[action]` is a single expiring flag. A second and third ATK tap during one swing both
wrote the same `light=true` slot, so three quick presses became only the current attack plus one
follow-up. Separately, the 24-damage light finisher reused `swingHeavy()` and crossed a generic
`dmg > 20` heavy-impact threshold, making the light string feel like repeated heavy attacks.

## Input and feedback contract

- A capped two-entry, light-only queue preserves exactly the two follow-ups in the authored string.
- Roll retains first priority at a state transition.
- Roll, heavy, damage, insufficient stamina, and combo expiry clear queued light follow-ups.
- The finisher remains 24 damage with its existing range, timing, lunge, knockback, hit-stop, and
  camera response.
- The finisher uses the light swing/contact family plus a silver damage number.
- HVY alone uses heavy swing/contact cues and the gold impact identity.

## Acceptance

The local production build, lint gate, and complete Playwright suite pass with zero errors.
Desktop deterministic simulation and a true-touch 390×844 browser each issue three real ATK
presses at 50 ms spacing and prove:

- three hits occur in order as steps `0, 1, 2`;
- every strike is marked non-heavy;
- the captured cues are `swing-0`, `hit-light-0`, `swing-1`, `hit-light-1`, `swing-2`,
  `hit-light-2`;
- no heavy cue occurs;
- the follow-up queue is empty after the string.

The wider gate retains difficulty, boss patterns, perfect dodge, hit-stop buffering, damage,
victory persistence, death/retry, accessibility, visual, audio, desktop, mobile, and true-touch
coverage.

## Changed from v2.11.1

- Rapid ATK input can no longer collapse three presses into two attacks.
- The light finisher no longer borrows the heavy attack's audio/visual identity.
- No balance value, boss behavior, difficulty, collision, save schema, UI layout, music, or
  runtime art asset changes.
